Sherlock Holmes sat by the window of 221B Baker Street, his eyes fixed on the grey London fog. He had not slept in two days, and the tobacco smoke hung heavy in the room.

Dr. Watson entered, his old war wound aching in his left shoulder. He had served in Afghanistan, and the Jezail bullet had left its mark on him forever.

"You look troubled, Holmes," said Watson, settling into his armchair. The detective merely nodded, still watching the street below.

Later that evening, Watson rubbed his leg, the very same wound now troubling him in a different limb entirely. Holmes noticed but said nothing of the curious inconsistency.
